My Work

Orchestra Vitae

I’ve recently designed and built a bespoke website for Orchestra Vitae, a charitable organization that helps young people through music. I gained a great deal of programming experience from this project however I also learned how to communicate effectively with a client. I had to bring their ideas and my ideas together to complete and finish the product. I also had to constantly adapt and change the build depending on their new requirements and find new ways of implementing features into the product. The project started as a freelance piece of work however the organization has gone on to employ me to maintain and upload new content to the site along with other members of their team. Just click on this link if you want to check out the website yourself - www.orchestravitae.co.uk

Maya Tank Project

This is model of a WW2 German tank (Panzer IV ASUFD) I built from scratch using Autodesk Maya, a 3D animation and modelling software. This was for a 3D Animation and Modelling module on my course. The project itself was to produce a model and a background environment working from a story board and concept art I had previously made. I wasn’t familiar with this software before this project, however by the end of it I learned how to model, animate, rig characters and vehicles.

Interface Project

This interface project was built for a Design for Digital Media module. The brief was to design and build an interface using Adobe Animate CC. I decided on building my interface based around the idea of it being used in a museum exhibit, as one of my passions outside of University is History. In this case the theme was the Roman Conquest of Britain. Every asset was and hand-drawn using my graphics tablet. in Adobe Illustrator. Although Adobe Flash is rapidly becoming obsolete, this project taught me the basics of animation. The knowledge I gained from this project is going on to help me this year with other projects using SVG animation.

Website High Fidelity Prototypes

These High-Fidelity prototypes were another project for my Design for Digital Media module. They were designed for to demonstrate a critical understanding of the principles of visual design for digital media. These were created in Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator for three web applications. The first one is ‘Coffee Talk’, a blog-style website where people share tips and tricks on different coffee recipes. The second one is ‘Scan Networking’ which is an e-commerce/business website for a company that would build and host websites and the third one is the ‘Woodsman’ website, a page created for an indie folk band. The knowledge gained by building these designs would go on to help me build prototypes and mock-ups the client websites and future university work.
